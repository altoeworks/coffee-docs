Hi, I'm Oscar. Hi, I'm Oscar.

I'm not a barista, a roaster, or anyone working in the coffee industry. I just got curious — and never really stopped.

It started with a simple question: why does my coffee sometimes taste incredible, and other times… not? That led to a lot of trial and error, research, experiments, more coffee than I care to admit, and eventually, writing. Not because I set out to make something public — but because putting it into words helped me make sense of it.

Along the way, I figured I wasn't the only one asking these questions. So I started turning what I'd learned into something I could share.

There's already plenty of gatekeeping in coffee. I wanted to make something useful without requiring a dictionary, a refractometer, or a stack of YouTube tabs. If you're curious — at any level — this is for you.

I'm not the most experienced person in the room, and that's fine. But if my way of explaining things helps something finally click for you, then it was worth the effort.

Coffee gets more enjoyable the more you understand it. And learning is always better when it's shared. If these guides help you taste something new, fix a frustrating shot, or just feel a little more confident about what's in your cup — that's more than enough.

I'm really glad you found this — whether by accident, curiosity, or deep caffeine-fueled investigation. I hope something here helps you notice more, understand a bit better, skip what doesn't serve you (for now or forever), and maybe enjoy your next cup a little more because of it.

Have fun with it.

The Guides

The Anatomy of Coffee Flavor – A Guide for Curious Drinkers

Why does coffee taste the way it does?

This guide is my attempt to answer that — from the ground up. It covers the main things that shape flavor: where coffee is grown, how it's processed, how it's roasted, brewed, and eventually tasted.

If you're just getting into specialty coffee, you'll find short summaries at the start of each section, clear explanations, and a glossary to help with unfamiliar terms. If you've been in it for a while, there's depth here too: from roast chemistry to fermentation types, water composition, variety differences, and sensory language.

I wrote it to make coffee a little easier to understand — whether you're new, already obsessed, or somewhere in between. It's not about telling you what's good. It's about helping you make sense of what you're tasting.

Under Pressure – The Technique, Taste, and Chaos of Espresso

What makes a good espresso — and why is it so easy to mess up?

This guide is for anyone who wants to understand espresso more deeply — whether you're just starting to dial in or already tracking shot curves and flow rates.

It breaks down all the key variables: grind size, dose, puck prep, tamping, pressure profiles, flow rate, shot time, yield. There's a full section on puck prep tools and routines, one on pressure and flow profiling (with actual graphs), a guide to diagnosing what went wrong in a shot, and another on mods and accessories — plus which ones might not be worth chasing.

It covers the variables that actually affect flavor: grind size, puck prep, pressure, flow, temperature, time, yield, and how all of it interacts. There's a full section on prep routines and tools, one on pressure profiling (with real examples), a guide to diagnosing weird shots, and a breakdown of useful (and maybe not-so-useful) accessories.

It's written to help you build a framework — not a checklist. Something you can refer back to when things are working, and especially when they're not.

If you're just learning, it'll give you clarity and confidence. If you're further along, it'll help organize what you already know and give you a few new ways to think about the process.
