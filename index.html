I'm not a barista, a roaster, or anyone working in the coffee industry. I just got curious — and never really stopped.

Skip to The Guides

It began with a simple question: why does my coffee sometimes taste amazing, and sometimes not? That curiosity led to experiments, research, tasting, failing, and adjusting. Eventually, I started writing. Not because I planned to — but because I needed to make sense of it all.

At some point, I realized others might be on the same path. So I started shaping what I'd learned into something I could share.

There's plenty of gatekeeping in coffee. I wanted to make something open and approachable — something that made it easier to learn, without needing to know the lingo or own fancy equipment. If you're curious, you're in.

There are people who know more, do more, test more. I don't pretend to be the most experienced voice in the room. But if my way of explaining things helps something finally click — then it was worth writing.

I believe coffee gets more enjoyable the more you understand it. And learning is better when it's shared. If these guides help you taste something new, solve a brewing problem, or just feel more confident — that's all I hoped for.

The Guides

The Anatomy of Coffee Flavor – A Guide for Curious Drinkers

Why does coffee taste the way it does?

This guide is for anyone curious enough to ask — whether you're just stepping beyond supermarket blends or already deep into fermentation styles, varietals, and roast curves.

Structured to be both approachable and insightful, it walks you through the key forces that shape flavor: from origin and processing, to roasting, brewing, and tasting. Each chapter starts with a short summary to help you navigate the big picture, while the body of the guide dives deeper for those who want to know why things taste the way they do.

The Anatomy of Coffee Flavor is part reference, part conversation — written to demystify, not to dictate. It doesn't tell you what to like. It helps you understand what's in your cup — and how to taste it with more clarity, confidence, and joy.

Under Pressure – The Technique, Taste, and Chaos of Espresso

What exactly makes espresso good — and why is it so hard to get there?

This guide is a deep but approachable dive into the mechanics and mysteries of espresso. From grind size to flow rate, puck prep to pressure curves, we explore the key variables that shape extraction and flavor — and how to make sense of them without losing your mind.

It's not a recipe book or a list of rules. Instead, it's a map: a way to navigate the tension between precision and chaos that defines espresso at any level. Whether you're new to dialing in or neck-deep in refractometers, this guide aims to sharpen your intuition, not just your technique.

Written for the curious, the obsessed, and everyone in between — and free for anyone who wants to brew better.
